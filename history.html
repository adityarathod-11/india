History of India

India, one of the world's oldest civilizations, has a rich and diverse history that spans thousands of years. From the ancient Indus Valley Civilization to the Vedic period, followed by powerful empires like the Mauryas, Guptas, and Mughals, India has always been a land of culture, knowledge, and tradition. The colonial era under British rule significantly altered its course, leading to a vibrant freedom struggle that culminated in independence in 1947. Modern India is a sovereign, democratic republic with a deep-rooted past and a dynamic future.

Details about India

The name India is derived from the River Indus, which was historically important to the Indian civilization. The Persians, who invaded India in ancient times, referred to the region beyond the Indus River as Hindustan, and later, it evolved into "India" during colonial and Western interactions.

On the other hand, Bharat finds its origin in ancient Indian scriptures. It is named after the legendary emperor Bharata, who is mentioned in texts like the Mahabharata and the Puranas. The official name of the country in Hindi is Bharat, as recognized in the Indian Constitution: “India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States.”

Thus, both names — India and Bharat — represent the rich cultural and historical legacy of the nation.

History of Flags in India

1. Calcutta Flag (1906)

Three horizontal stripes: green, yellow, red. Eight lotuses, 'Vande Mataram' text, and symbols of sun and moon.

2. Berlin Committee Flag (1914)

Similar tricolor with sun, moon, and seven stars representing Saptarishi.

3. Home Rule Movement Flag (1917)

Five red and four green stripes, Union Jack, crescent, star, and seven stars of Saptarishi.

4. Flag of 1921 (Gandhi Flag)

Tricolor of red, white, and green with a spinning wheel (Charkha).

5. Swaraj Flag (1931)

Tricolor of saffron, white, and green with a spinning wheel in the center.

6. Current National Flag (1947–Present)

Saffron, white, green stripes with Ashoka Chakra in navy blue at the center.
